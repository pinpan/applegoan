Our Philosophy

We do see SW as both - a tool and spirit.
As a tool it should be selected or built carefully, then maintained properly and innovated regularly to serve its purpose and improve quality and productivity.

Spirit is even more important as it gives organization distinctstive value and advantage against competitors, who use similar tools. Even best possible tool can be dangerous or at least not usefull if it doesn't reflect the organization philosophy and strategy. Does it mean that each and every organization has to own different custom made SW? No! SW must be written in such a way to serve and power multiple businesses as long as possible, provided that it can be fine tuned and adapted to concrete models and processes.

We do believe that success is a function of knowledge, experience and a lot of hard work.

To rephrase Mr. Thomas Eddison's sentence:"Opportunity is missed by most people because it is dressed in overalls and looks like work."

We do believe that writing new SW applications everytime when new feature is needed, is environment unfrendly and dangerous behavior. This way organizations often operate feature redundant systems which even if not used, reserve and consume power and resources.
  • Developing new piece of SW is expensive as of time and resources consumption. It takes 2 to 12 months in average to bring to live medium scale system.
  • Operating an other piece of SW is expensive as for energy consumption, corresponding HW reources allocation, maintenance and support Every single electron to power duplicated functionality is a environment killer.
  • Creating more and more SW, employes some of the best brains of the planet. Now imagine how much spirit and knowledge is wasted. These armadas could produce something more valuable.
It is not to say we don't love developing new SW. We often apeal to our customers to think wisely about evey feature proposal and its realization.
